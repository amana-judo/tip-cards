TIP 04

Stop asking "Does it make sense?"

The problem

After you pitch Port or run a demo/cold outreach, don't ask "Does it make sense?" — it's a dead-end question.

It makes it too easy for the customer to nod and say yes while holding back what they really think. Something can make perfect sense and still not be what they're looking for.

The shift
Don't ask this
Rep
Customer
"Does it make sense?"
"Yeah… sure." (holds back)
Deal goes quiet. You never find out why.
Ask this instead
Rep
Customer
"Does this check your boxes?"
"Yes, but I expected to see X."
Gap addressed while still on the call.
Why it works
That one small shift changes the entire dynamic. The answer you'll usually get is: "Yes, but I expected to see X." And that's exactly where you want to be — now you have a chance to address what's missing while you're still on the call, instead of finding out later why the deal went quiet.
Don't let customers leave politely confused. Make sure they leave having seen everything they came for.
